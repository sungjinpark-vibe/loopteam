## Brief

The director's brief (2026-07-16): *"lifetown 확인해서 같은 앱을 유니티로 만들되, 앱의 목적/목표를 더
잘 달성할 수 있게 만들어줘."* — rebuild Life Town in Unity, but **make it better achieve its purpose.
Not a straight port.**

Produce the spec for that rebuild. The hard part is not "what did the Flutter app have"; it is
**"what should the Unity version be, so the purpose is actually served?"** — and then an MVP scope the
director can say yes to.

### The purpose (do not drift from this)
> *"보이지 않는 노력(집중 시간)을 가시적 성취(마을)로 전환해 습관 형성을 돕는다"* —
> **"내 시간과 노력을 눈에 보이게."** (`docs/spec/00-overview.md` §2)

And the **superseding identity** (`07-decisions-locked.md`, director-approved 2026-07-05, declared final
over conflicting specs): Life Town is a **life-logging** app that mirrors life *neutrally*, **leisure
included**. It is NOT a self-improvement app that only counts productive hours. Growth and leisure
categories both build the village; the report shows the balance and lets the player notice it.

**Any proposal that turns this back into a productivity/self-improvement app contradicts a locked
director decision.** If you want to argue against it, say so explicitly as a decision for the director —
do not quietly drift.

### The most important fact you have been given
The original's **integrity system does not exist**. `lib/core/economy/economy.dart:10-12` states plainly
that daily caps and focus-enforcement are out of scope; only a 60-second minimum survives. There is **no
server at all** (no Cloud Functions), so the client is fully authoritative — session commit, caps,
clock-tamper defense, idempotency are all specced as Must and all absent.

**If the timer can be cheated, the village stops mirroring a real life and becomes decoration — the
purpose collapses.** Consider seriously whether trustworthiness does more for the purpose than any new
feature. But **argue it; do not assume it.** Your angle decides how hard you push this.

## Acceptance criteria
- [ ] States the **MVP scope explicitly**, as a list of what is IN and what is deliberately OUT. The
      original is 34k lines / 27 screens — a proposal that implies "port all of it" without saying so
      is not scoped, and will be scored down on P4 (scope honesty).
- [ ] Defines the **core loop** as an actual loop (timer → EXP/coin → build/level/merge → see your
      life → return), concrete enough for `client-dev` to start without asking a question (P2).
- [ ] Takes an explicit, argued position on **integrity/trust** — what makes the village believable,
      and what that costs. Silence on this is a P1 failure (fidelity to intent).
- [ ] Says what **Unity buys us that Flutter did not** — and is honest where the answer is "nothing".
      The stack is fixed by director rule; do not re-litigate it, but do exploit it.
- [ ] Specifies **screens** (name, purpose, key elements, navigation) for the MVP scope only.
- [ ] Specifies **data structures** for the MVP scope.
- [ ] Lists **open decisions the director must make**, separated from your assumptions (P5).
- [ ] Marks every assumption as an assumption.
